Wage Theft: Why is it important?

Wage Theft by definition is the failure to compensate a worker for their services. Despite being against the law,
employees continue to suffer minimum wage, overtime, and meal break violations amidst many other forms.
Underpaid minimum wage workers specifically lose an average of $64 per week, losing about $3,300 per year.
Wage theft is mostly common among young workers, women, immigrant workers, and minorities who have
trouble standing up for themselves. According to the Economic Policy Institute, "not only do those workers and
their families suffer, but the public is harmed as well—the government collects less in tax revenue, and taxpayers
must provide additional funding for social welfare programs to fill in the gaps that employers created." In order
to help reduce the amount of wage theft victims our group, Under Construction, has created a social tool to
connect employees with legal options, resources, and other workers who have experienced similar situations.
We have also created a digital calculator to help keep track of your daily income in order to compare your
estimated total with your actual check. Our built in alerts will notify you if there is any significant difference
that may require you to talk to your boss/managers and any legal alternatives.
